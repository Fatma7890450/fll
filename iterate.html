User Testing & Feedback

Field Testing

To improve the design of the TechnoRelics system, the prototype was shared with users to simulate real archaeological field conditions. The testing revealed differences in user ability to operate the robot, highlighting the need for a simpler and more intuitive interface.

Usability Challenges

One participant struggled due to unclear instructions, while another required direct guidance. This emphasized the importance of a menu-based control system for easy access to core functions such as movement, scanning, and documentation.

Safety & Maintenance

Users raised concerns about maintenance and potential damage to artifacts. A safety alarm was triggered during excavator arm movement, indicating the need to refine safety zones and improve visual and audio warnings.

Survey Results

Technology Acceptance

Most participants were aware of archaeological robotics and supported the use of modern technology in excavation projects.

Key Priorities

Accuracy and artifact protection were identified as the most critical system features for successful field deployment.

Main Concerns

Participants expressed concerns about artifact safety, system complexity, and overall project cost.

Deployment in Egypt

Respondents confirmed that the system is suitable for deployment at Egyptian archaeological sites, with recommendations for clearer instructions and improved safety measures.

Proposed Improvements

Interface Design

Develop a more intuitive, menu-based user interface to simplify robot operation.

Operating Instructions

Provide clear and easy-to-follow operating instructions for all users.

Enhanced Safety

Enhance safety systems and alerts around moving parts to protect users and artifacts.

Maintenance Guide

Include a basic maintenance guide to allow routine checks and minor adjustments without specialist support.
